# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three named audiences (per `Brand_Messaging_Framework.md`): Hispanic communities (family, celebration, togetherness), Black communities (connection, authenticity, good energy), and the working-class audience (stress relief, value, escape). Shared context: hard-working Wicker Park / West Town locals, 21+, mostly on mobile, who have just heard of The Flame and are deciding whether to walk in. The job to be done: "is this place for me, what actually happens there, and is it easy/safe/cheap to try?"

## Product Purpose

Single-page brand site for theflamechicago.com — a smoke-free neighborhood sweepstakes gaming lounge at 1231 N Ashland Ave. The site's job is to convert first-time awareness into a first visit (directions/call), replacing the old cash-payout template site. Success = a first-timer understands what The Flame is, feels welcome, and gets directions. Must be live before Wicker Park Fest, July 24 2026.

## Brand Personality

Welcoming, relaxed, celebratory. Tagline: **Relax. Play. Connect.** Master message: hard-working people deserve a place to take a break that feels like home. Four pillars: People, Winners (emotional, not financial), Atmosphere, Experience. Premium = lighting, not brightness — warm, moody, glowing.

## Anti-references

- The pre-rebrand site (see `Dropbox/Public/The Flame Chicago/website-audit/Website_Audit.md`): fire graphics, neon slot-hall energy, "SPIN🔥WIN", cash-prize language.
- Framework "We Are Not": corporate, exclusive, flashy, aggressive, gambling-focused, get-rich-quick, overly promotional.
- Language to avoid: get rich, easy money, big payouts, guaranteed wins, cash machine, jackpot destination, win big every time.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with people and atmosphere; gaming supports the story, never defines it.
2. Every asset must read as THIS room — the real storefront, cabinets, and neon flame lamp; premium comes from lighting and framing, not invention.
3. Answer the first-timer's anxieties out loud (cost, membership, smoke, what to do) — reassurance is the conversion strategy.
4. Neighborhood-first: Wicker Park / West Town named, transit and parking practical, bilingual plan (EN/ES).
5. Warm, plain, human copy — short sentences, no hype, no hedging.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA floor: skip link, focus-visible, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored. Bilingual EN/ES planned (Accept-Language, no manual toggle per client). Mobile-first — sticky call/directions bar.
