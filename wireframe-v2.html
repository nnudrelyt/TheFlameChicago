The Flame — Website v2

Same brand and copy foundation as v1, re-shaped around one ideal customer and one goal: turn a first-time visitor into a regular, and turn intent into a walk-in tonight. Low-fidelity — boxes and copy direction, not final visuals.

Designing for · The Neighborhood Regular

30–55, lives or works within a mile of Ashland & Division. Shift or long hours — trades, service, healthcare, retail. Wants a low-key third place to decompress after work: not a loud bar, not an intimidating casino. Comes back for familiarity, value, and being recognized.

First-visit fears we must kill

  • "Is this a gambling den?"
  • "Is it actually for someone like me?"
  • "Do I need money to enjoy it?"
  • "Will I feel out of place walking in?"

To build it — needs & open calls

  • Confirm ICP = the working neighborhood regular (vs. leaning a Hispanic-family or Black-community angle — shifts copy & imagery)
  • 3–5 real regular quotes / Google reviews + first names
  • Room photos (or generated-to-this-room imagery)
  • Confirm full weekly hours for the "Open now" logic
  • Keep the same warm-dark Flame visual system from v1 in final design
